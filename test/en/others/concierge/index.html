May 4 (Sat.)

★ Miyuki Fujita

CHIKI-CHIKI (1-5-30 Ohiraki, Fukushima, Osaka)

Some call her “Jeanne d’Arc of Sake,” respecting her style of popularizing Nama-genshu (unpasteurized and undiluted sake) in the corner of Noda, Osaka. She, the “martyr of Namagenshu,” will recommend you a wide range of sake from Nama-genshu to Hi-ire (pasteurized and/ or diluted sake)

★ Charles Genki Shimokura

Former 3rd president of Osaka University Sake Circle

He is a young Sake missionary who was involved in producing the beginner-oriented Jyunmai- Ginjyo Sake “hajime.” Please check out a brand-new chapter of Sake in the history of “Reiwa,” edited by his “Heisei-born” sound sensitivity, with your own eyes!

May 5 (Sun.)

★Mayu Miyamura

Benikurage (5-4 Honmachi-bashi, Chuo, Osaka)

Some call her “less-attractive Marilyn Monroe of Sake,” respecting her style of fascinating gentlemen with her gorgeous figure and innocence by nature. Although she “likes it hot,” she will recommend you a wide range of sake from Reishu (chilled sake) to Kanshu (hot sake)!

★Takashi Eguchi (aka Umio)

Representative of Sake Concierge News

He had traveled to Paris, São Paulo, and Taipei last year. He is a Sake hunter who runs around the world to keep himself updated to the latest Sake affairs. By his intelligent and charitable selection of Sake, Minatomachi River Place will transform into “the globe!”
